The Culture of Conventions

The dynamic world of pop culture conventions emphasizes the art of cosplay. This article also highlights the popularity of said hobby in the Philippines, and stresses its inclusive nature as a leisure activity for everyone.

PAGE 06

"Cosplay is Not Consent": Its History

The "Cosplay is Not Consent" movement, originating from the United States, tackles the widespread issue of cosplayers experiencing unwarranted attention, focusing the crucial values of respect, personal space, and consent within the convention community.

PAGE 08

The Unconventional Designs of Characters

There is a problematic trend of turning fictional characters, particularly females, as instruments to fuel sex appeal in popular media. It contributes to issues such as reinforcing stereotypes, undermining the seriousness of characters, and developing toxic environments within fan communities.

PAGE 10

Not Touching Grass and its Delusional Effects

As consumers of media, we do sometimes form emotional connections with fictional characters. This article discusses the reasons behind these attachments, the potential consequences, and how one’s lifestyle contributes to the line between fiction and reality being blurred.

PAGE 12

Issues Faced by Female Cosplayers

Female cosplayers experience body - shaming and unwanted attention countless times at conventions. They feel the impact of the unrealistic representations of female characters in popular media and the persistence of harassment within the cosplay community.

PAGE 14

Saying NO! For New Cosplayers

As a cosplayer, a lot of people will take photos, request poses, and many more from you. This article gives some ideas about setting boundaries and to NOT tolerate people who continue to violate it.

PAGE 18

Photos Shown First!!

While asking for permission to take pictures is common knowledge, there are certain people who technically get consent to take photos but take advantage of it. Watch out for creepy people who take close up shots of intimate areas and stand your ground for such situations.

PAGE 20

Investigations Before Events

The cosplay and convention community are active on social media and they often have conversations about which people to avoid. Try to browse and protect yourself from such people not just within the convention venue but outside as well.

PAGE 22

Thinking Ahead Through Social Media

Cosplayers and convention goers who are active online often announce ahead of time which convention they are going to and their own boundaries. Get an idea of boundaries you can set ahead of time to your online friends and fellow convention goers!

PAGE 24

Cosplayer Stories: The Bad

Learn more about the challenges faced by the cosplay community from the cosplayers themselves by reading about their unpleasant experiences at conventions in this article.

PAGE 28

Cosplayer Stories: The Good

Cosplaying at conventions is a lot of fun! Be inspired by the stories of cosplayers who shared their greatest and most memorable cosplaying experiences with us.
