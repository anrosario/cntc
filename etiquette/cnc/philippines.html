CONVENTION ETIQUETTE

“Cosplay is Not Consent”

in the Philippines

With the growing popularity of the cosplay hobby in the Philippines in recent years, the issue of inappropriate behavior toward cosplayers, which are mostly sexual in nature, have been brought to light. Our team conducted research to gather insights from convention attendees, experienced cosplayers, and organizers regarding the campaign and related issues.

Acts of misbehavior and sexual harassment compromise the safety and well-being of cosplayers.

These are significant issues within Philippine pop culture conventions. In fact, many convention goers have heard or witnessed such incidents, making them feel uneasy.

87%

HAVE KNOWLEDGE OF A MISBEHAVIOR OR SEXUAL HARASSMENT INCIDENT AT A CONVENTION

81%

ARE AWARE OF THE “COSPLAY IS NOT CONSENT” MOVEMENT
PERCEPTION ON “COSPLAY IS NOT CONSENT”

Raises awareness but should be promoted more effectively

The data above is based on a survey of 63 convention goers from our study on the effectiveness of the “Cosplay is Not Consent” movement.
Read our study here.
To address the issue, the dissemination and enforcement of the campaign and convention policies should be improved.

The convention goers in the Philippines are aware of "Cosplay is Not Consent" and local organizers remind them about it prior to events. However, with more pop culture conventions happening, there may be newcomers who are unaware of the proper etiquette when interacting with cosplayers. Also, cosplayers still encounter harassment at these events despite reminders from the organizers.
