CONVENTION ETIQUETTE

Organizers' Perspective

Pop culture conventions are events where you can enjoy your hobbies with your fellow convention goers. Convention organizers ensure that their events are orderly and pleasant through the implementation of convention policies and security protocols.

On “Cosplay is Not Consent”

The "Cosplay is Not Consent" campaign is not as common in the Philippines as in the United States, but local organizers are aware of it due to the rise in sexual harassment incidents here. They emphasize respecting cosplayers' boundaries by posting reminders on social media before their events.

This image illustrates an internal protocol of organizers in which they have marshals who discreetly monitor perverts and photographers who may act inappropriately toward other people.

Anti-sexual harassment policies

Representatives from a major local convention organizer shared with us that they prioritize the safety of all convention goers regardless of whether they are cosplayers, celebrity guests, or regular attendees. Their security team is aware of the issues encountered by cosplayers and accommodates their concerns as much as possible.

WHAT THEY DO
Implement a "Cosplayer First" policy to prioritize the safety of cosplayers.

A staff team is dedicated to managing the cosplay aspects of the events, including providing cosplayers with a separate baggage area.

Security teams follow internal protocols.

They keep a list of known perverts to monitor at conventions. Marshals discreetly roam the venue to watch for inappropriate behavior toward attendees.

Respond to complaints that are actionable.

The security team requires sufficient proof of the incident before responding to complaints. The police may intervene if necessary.
