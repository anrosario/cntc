CONVENTION ETIQUETTE

Effectiveness of the “Cosplay is Not Consent” Campaign and Related Policies

Are the actions taken by organizers enough to address the issues of misbehavior and sexual harassment at pop culture conventions? While the campaign is acknowledged by the majority of the community and is incorporated by organizers into their policies, it is inadequate and ineffective.

Why are the campaign and related policies inadequate and ineffective?

Lack of information in social media posts

Posts by convention organizers include the phrase "Cosplay is Not Consent" without further context or guidelines.

Lack of anti-sexual harassment signs and other printed materials onsite

Physical signs are not always present at the venues. Convention goers are reminded through live announcements instead.

Lack of guidelines and illustrations regarding convention etiquette

Convention policies do not include clear information as to what entails sexual harassment. Convention goers are not properly informed about rules and consequences regarding misbehavior and sexual harassment.

Lack of proper implementation of convention rules and regulations

Rules on sexual harassment are not enforced properly and reports of incidents are not handled effectively by convention organizers.

This image depicts the organizers' efforts in promoting the "Cosplay is Not Consent" movement as part of their convention policies.

How can organizers improve the campaign and their policies?

Better dissemination of policies through social media platforms

Rather than simply stating “Cosplay is Not Consent”, include specific guidelines such as prohibited actions and items, and penalties for violations, if applicable.

Addition of physical materials and signages onsite regarding “Cosplay is Not Consent”

Some convention goers may be unaware of the policies before the event, so it is important to use signages and distribute physical materials to remind them onsite.

Clearly define what constitutes misbehavior and sexual harassment

Educate convention goers about convention guidelines through clear messages. Add visual aids to engage and help convention goers remember important information.

Proper implementation and enforcement of rules and regulations

Ensure convention goers are well-informed of the policies through effective online and onsite dissemination. Train staff and volunteers accordingly.
