CONVENTION ETIQUETTE

Cosplay is Not Consent

“Cosplay is Not Consent” is one of the golden rules of cosplay. It raises awareness on a common issue experienced by cosplayers. What does it mean and how did it emerge?

The idea is that
A costume is not an open invitation to the cosplayer’s body.
Cosplayers are not the fictional characters they portray. They are real people whose boundaries should be respected.

How did it emerge?

The movement was initiated to combat sexual harassment and misbehavior toward cosplayers.

Pop culture conventions are spaces where fan communities socialize, express themselves, meet artists, and buy merchandise. However, many cosplayers have faced sexual harassment at these events, leading to the "Cosplay is Not Consent" movement, which began in 2013. Now, it has become a popular catchphrase and common policy within the community, including the Philippines.

What is sexual harassment?

Sexual harassment is generally defined as an “uninvited and unwelcome verbal or physical behavior of a sexual nature”.

REMINDER TO ALL CONVENTION GOERS

Report incidents of misbehavior and sexual harassment immediately to a staff during the event and present supporting evidence.

This allows authorized personnel to respond promptly and effectively and prevent escalation of the issue and future incidents. Victims may also seek assistance at a police precinct, local government unit, and public attorney’s office if their report is not handled properly at the event.

Why do these incidents happen?

Male dominance and portrayal of women in media contribute to the issue.

Most victims at conventions are female cosplayers, reflecting male dominance and exclusion of women in these spaces. Heavy consumption of fictional media can also blur the lines between fiction and reality, leading men to feel entitled to a cosplayer’s body. Movies, games, comics, and other forms of media often portray women as sexual objects, focusing on their physical appearance.

Male cosplayers and anyone attending conventions can also be victims of misbehavior and sexual harassment, so it is important to always be mindful, respect boundaries, and follow guidelines.
