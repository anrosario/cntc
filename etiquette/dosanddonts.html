CONVENTION ETIQUETTE

Dos and Don'ts

Learn about the dos and don'ts when interacting with cosplayers. The general rule is to always ask for permission first. Listed below are situations wherein permission is needed such as taking photos and touching props.

DOS

DO ask for permission first before taking a photo of the cosplayer, especially if they are the main focus of the photo.
DO ask for permission first before touching/playing/grabbing a cosplayer’s props.

Handle their props with care, as they have entrusted them to you.

DO ask first before doing any intimate pose with the cosplayer.

If the cosplayer rejects your request, PLEASE choose a different pose to respect their boundaries.

Overall general rule is to always ask a cosplayer before taking a photo, asking for a pose, requesting to touch their equipment, etc.

DON'TS

Do NOT take photos of cosplayers, specifically if they are the main subject, WITHOUT permission.
Do NOT purposefully grab/play with a cosplayer’s props/costume/equipment WITHOUT permission.
Do NOT put your hands around their shoulders, hug, or any sort of intimate pose WITHOUT permission.

It is ideal to clearly state the pose or action you have in mind to the cosplayer so that they can fully understand and respond appropriately.

Do NOT take photos of cosplayers that focus on their intimate areas, regardless if they give permission.

REMINDER TO ALL CONVENTION GOERS

Report incidents of misbehavior and sexual harassment immediately to a staff during the event and present supporting evidence.

This allows authorized personnel to respond promptly and effectively and prevent escalation of the issue and future incidents. Victims may also seek assistance at a police precinct, local government unit, and public attorney’s office if their report is not handled properly at the event.
